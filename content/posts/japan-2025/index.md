## Japan 2025
{{< lead >}}
Tuesday May 06, 2025 — Saturday May 19, 2025
{{< /lead >}}

Join me for my 'Tokyo/Osaka 2025' journey! This travel blog covers my experience competing at Evo Japan in Street Fighter 6 and Tekken 8, alongside a deep dive into Japanese food culture. From upscale business class flights and Green Car Shinkansen rides to exploring Tokyo's major hubs and tasting Osaka's finest cuisine like Gyukatsu and soufflé pancakes, this is the ultimate travel diary for the modern gamer and foodie.

### Day 1: High Skies, High Hopes, and a Very Awake Brain
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_1" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

My trip to Japan started with a Lyft ride to JFK. Upon arrival, I proceeded to the Greenwich Lounge, where I awaited confirmation of my bid upgrade from Premium Economy to Business Class on Japan Airlines, which was successful.

The flight, JL5 on the A350-1000, boarded on time. I appreciated the comfort of business class and the quality of the in-flight dining. Despite the comfortable environment, I found it difficult to sleep, possibly due to insomnia attributed to Sudafed I had been taking. Regardless, the journey was smooth, and the in-flight service was commendable.

My arrival in Tokyo was smooth. After clearing customs, I picked up a Pasmo card from a machine by the Keisei line at the airport. From there, it was a multi-leg trip to the La Vista hotel, taking the Monorail, Rinkai line, and then the Yurikamome line.

Later, I met up with Abdul for dinner at Tsukishima Monja Jugoya in Toyosu Banrai. It was my first experience with monjayaki, and watching the staff prepare it right at our table was a pretty cool way to kick off the culinary adventures. After dinner, I headed to bed.

### Day 2: Shopping in Tokyo
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_2" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

After a satisfying hotel breakfast buffet, I made my way to Shibuya for some much-needed retail therapy. Amidst the bustling crowds and flashing neon, I managed to track down some specific hot sauce for Chives at a vending machine – a true Tokyo success story!

As evening approached, I linked up with Abdul for dinner at Fry-ya by Takadanobaba. It's always great to catch up with friends over good food.

Later, we met up with Eniola at Bar B&F in Shinjuku for drinks. It had been a while since I'd seen him, so it was fantastic to reconnect and swap stories in one of Tokyo's most vibrant districts.

### Day 3: Evo Japan
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_3" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

My morning started with a quick ride on the Yurikamome line to Tokyo Big Sight, the colossal convention center playing host to Evo Japan for my SF6 and Tekken 8 pools. I was hyped to jump into my Street Fighter 6 matches, but unfortunately, it wasn't my day. I went 0-2, first falling to a Japanese Jamie player, then to a Japanese Guile player. Tough crowd!


I grabbed a quick lunch at the Familymart at the venue – an Amerikan Doggu and Famichiki hit the spot.

Just before my Tekken pools, the jet lag hit hard. I was incredibly tired, and even a Red Bull from one of the vending girls roaming the venue wasn't enough to fully shake it off.

Despite the fatigue, I managed to win my first match against a Japanese Asuka player. However, my next two matches were against other foreigners – a Bryan and a Dragunov player. I lost both rock-paper-scissors (RPS) decisions, putting me as player 2 for both matches, and unfortunately, I lost both sets.

After the tournaments, I made my way back towards my hotel. I picked up a Nana Chiki from 7/11 for a late-night snack before finally crashing into bed. 

### Day 4: Shopping in Tokyo 2
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_4" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

My latest day in Tokyo kicked off with an unwelcome guest: a pounding headache. A quick trip to a nearby drugstore for some Bufferin Premium DX was my first mission of the day.

My original plan was to hit up Evo Japan, but after some consideration, I decided against it. Between a general disinterest in the current fighting game meta, the all-Japanese commentary, and a serious lack of seating, it felt like a better use of my time to just catch the English VODs later. Sometimes, comfort and convenience win out!

Later, I met up with Abdul in Shibuya for lunch. We grabbed some quick bites at Taco Bell – a taste of home in the heart of Tokyo.  The highlight here was the Frozen Calpico as they don't serve Baja Blast in Japan. Afterward, we spent some time wandering around the shops, chatting with Mariuxi on WhatsApp as we scoured secondhand stores for a purse for her. Despite our best efforts, we didn't quite find what she was looking for.

The evening brought a fantastic culinary experience. I had a dinner reservation at Shibuya Sushi Labo, and it turned out to be a truly outstanding omakase experience. For a really reasonable cost, around $100, I enjoyed a wide variety of fresh, delicious sushi. It was a perfect way to end the day – a delightful and well-priced treat for the taste buds.

### Day 5: More Shopping and Teamlab Planets
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_5" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

I met up with Abdul and we headed to Ikebukuro. We grabbed lunch at Coco Ichibanya in Sunshine City, a reliable choice for Japanese curry.

Afterward, we embarked on a fun tour of Sunshine City, exploring various pop culture havens. Later, we visited Teamlab Planets, an immersive digital art museum. For dinner, we enjoyed skewers and croquettes at 肉好き大黒天 at Toyosu Banrai.

### Day 6: Trading Tokyo for Osaka
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_6" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

My Japan adventure continues, and today marked a significant shift as I bid farewell to Tokyo and journeyed to the vibrant city of Osaka!

After checking out of my Tokyo hotel this morning, Abdul and I hopped on the Shinkansen from Tokyo Station. We splurged a little and enjoyed the front row of a Green Car, offering fantastic views and a supremely comfortable ride. For lunch, I indulged in a self-heating ekiben (train bento box) featuring delicious rice and beef tongue. I also couldn't resist trying the onboard ordering system to get a delectable Shine Muscat grape ice cream delivered right to my seat – a true highlight of Japanese train travel!

Upon arrival, we split up to check into our hotels in Osaka before meeting up with Chiping for dinner at a Gyukatsu spot. The crispy, juicy beef cutlets were a perfect welcome to Osaka's culinary scene. Afterward, we spent some time soaking in the lively atmosphere of Dotonbori and Shinsaibashi, taking in the iconic neon lights and bustling streets before heading back to the hotel.

### Day 7: Osaka Delights: Fluffy Pancakes, Futuristic Pavilions, and Crispy Tempura
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_7" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

Soufflé Pancake Perfection in Namba
This morning, I met up with Chiping and Abdul for what promised to be a delightful breakfast: Japanese soufflé pancakes at Cafe Annon Namba. We arrived a little late and missed the first seating, resulting in a 40+ minute wait after opening. But let me tell you, it was worth it! I enjoyed a refreshing cafe au lait float and devoured some truly fantastic strawberry banana pancakes. They were every bit as fluffy and delicious as they looked.

A Glimpse into the Future at Osaka Expo
After satisfying our pancake cravings, we headed to the Osaka Expo (https://www.expo2025.or.jp/en/) to explore the various pavilions. This gave us a fascinating glimpse into what the 2025 World Expo might be like! For me, the highlights were the fashion-focused France pavilion and the science-focused America pavilion. The American pavilion, in particular, reminded me of a tour through a science museum, much like the Pacific Science Center or the Exploratorium, with its interactive and educational exhibits. I also grabbed a Vietnamese coffee from the Vietnam pavilion, though it was a bit on the pricey side.

Tempura Excellence at OIMATSU Tempura Suzuki
The evening brought another fantastic culinary experience. Chiping, Abdul, and I had a dinner reservation at OIMATSU Tempura Suzuki. The food was excellent – each piece of tempura perfectly crisp and flavorful. The service was equally impressive, making for a truly enjoyable meal to round out the day.

Osaka continues to impress with its vibrant food scene and diverse attractions!

### Day 8: Nara Daytrip and more Osaka
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_8" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

The day began with Abdul and me heading to Kuromon Market for breakfast. I enjoyed a delicious croissant with sausage and coffee amidst the bustling market atmosphere.

After breakfast, we embarked on a day trip to Nara to visit the famous deer. It's always a unique experience to interact with the bowing deer! While Chiping spent the day working, Abdul and I explored Nara Park, enjoying the peaceful surroundings and the friendly deer.

For lunch, we grabbed a bite at Mos Burger, where I tried their rice yakiniku burger. Afterward, we indulged in some freshly pounded mochi for dessert before splitting up for the afternoon.

Rhythm Games and Yakiniku with Friends
After my session, I headed to Gigo Namba Avion to play some PIU (Pump It Up). I even met a lady from Indonesia and we played a set of PIU singles together.

Later, I met up with Chiping and Abdul for dinner at Ushinokura Namba for some fantastic Yakiniku. The grilled meats were a perfect end to a busy day.

After dinner, Chiping and I returned to Gigo to play some old, removed PIU Prime 2 co-ops and other rhythm games. We then linked back up with Abdul at Maccha House for dessert. Our evening concluded with a stop at Round1, where we spotted the legendary DDR player, O4MA!

### Day 9: Universal Studios
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_9" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

Today was a highly anticipated day as I finally got to experience the magic of Universal Studios Japan (USJ) in Osaka with Chiping, eager to explore Nintendo World and Harry Potter World.

Unfortunately, my most anticipated ride, Flying Dinosaur, was down for repairs today – a real bummer! Since Chiping had time-specific express passes, I hung out with him until my own VIP tour at 3 PM. This was my first VIP experience at Universal, and it was genuinely fun! Our tour guide, George, was a fantastic host. Unlike express passes, the VIP tour takes you through dedicated lines or even backstage routes for the rides, which is a neat perk. The only downside during the tour was that Yoshi's Adventure broke down just as we were near the front of the line. Also, by not going through the regular queues, you do miss out on some of the cool theming within the lines.

Abdul had separate dinner plans, so Chiping and I grabbed dinner inside the park. I also managed to convince Chiping to try Gongcha, which he absolutely hated – a little victory for me, even if it wasn't for him!

### Day 10: Back to Tokyo
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_10" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

My Japanese journey has come full circle as I've returned to the bustling heart of Tokyo after an exciting stint in Osaka.

My day began with a rather decadent breakfast at the Talk Shop restaurant at the Hyatt, where I indulged in a delicious Wagyu beef breakfast set. What a way to start the day!

After checking out of the Hyatt, I met up with Chiping and Abdul, and we boarded the Shinkansen back to Tokyo. We once again secured those coveted front row seats in the Green Car, making for a very comfortable and scenic journey. Chiping thoughtfully brought some cheesecake to share, and I enjoyed another tasty Ekiben for lunch on the ride.

Upon arriving back in Tokyo, I checked into the luxurious Conrad Tokyo.

The day concluded with a fantastic dinner with Chiping at La Rochelle, the renowned French restaurant by Iron Chef Sakai. The meal was absolutely superb, a truly great fine dining experience to cap off my return to Tokyo.

It's good to be back in Tokyo, enjoying both its familiar comforts and new experiences!

### Day 12: Akihabara
---
{{< gallery >}}
  {{< oci_gallery day="day_12" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

After a delicious avocado toast for breakfast at the hotel, I headed to Akihabara for the morning and afternoon with Chiping. Unfortunately, Abdul, who was supposed to join us, ended up sleeping in before heading off for his flight back to Vancouver – safe travels, Abdul!

Chiping and I grabbed lunch at Sushiro, which was running a cool collaboration with the Hoyoverse game, Honkai Star Rail. What's surprising is that we spent around six hours in Akihabara, the electric town, just Browse and somehow didn't buy anything! It made me ponder if perhaps I'm no longer the "weeb" I once thought I was. We also stopped by a Cocos for bubble tea, and to my amusement, Chiping disliked it just as much as the Gongcha we had earlier this trip.

For dinner, I decided to use my FHR (Fine Hotels & Resorts) dining credit at the Kazahana restaurant in the Conrad. This marked my first truly nice Teppanyaki experience, and it was absolutely fantastic. The A5 Wagyu was incredibly delicious and wonderfully filling. It was a perfect way to cap off a day of urban exploration.

### Day 13: Flight back to NYC
---

  {{< oci_gallery day="day_13" >}}
{{< /gallery >}}

I stopped by Uniqlo in Ginza to pick up some last-minute items. However, to my dismay, I realized I had left my passport back at the hotel, meaning I couldn't take advantage of the tax-free shopping. A minor setback, but a lesson learned for next time!

Finally, it was time to head to the airport. I relaxed in the Sakura Lounge until it was time to board my flight, marking the end of an unforgettable trip. From the bustling streets of Tokyo to the serene deer of Nara, the vibrant nightlife of Osaka, and all the unique encounters in between, this solo adventure has been nothing short of extraordinary. Japan, you've been incredible, and I'm already looking forward to my next visit!
